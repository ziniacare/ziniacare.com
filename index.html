ZiniaCare. Göteborg · Mölndal · Borås

Rehabilitation technology for recovery at home.

ZiniaCare AB is a Swedish medical-technology company developing the ZiniaCare System — a biofeedback system for home-based hand and forearm rehabilitation for adults recovering from stroke or brain injury.

What we are building

The ZiniaCare System uses surface electromyography (sEMG) — muscle-activity signals read through a textile sleeve worn on the forearm — to give patients real-time visual and auditory biofeedback while they train, and to let clinicians follow progress between visits.

It is designed for use at home, by the patient, without direct supervision by a physiotherapist — so rehabilitation can continue where everyday life happens.

Status: the ZiniaCare System is under development and is being prepared for conformity assessment under the EU Medical Device Regulation (MDR 2017/745) and premarket review by the U.S. FDA. It is not yet CE marked and not available for sale.

Origin & research

ZiniaCare grew out of a collaboration between Daralabs, Chalmers University of Technology and the Swedish School of Textiles at the University of Borås — sEMG rehabilitation R&D that began in the mid-2010s and was spun out as ZiniaCare AB in 2025. The company is rooted in Göteborg, Mölndal and Borås.

In 2026, the co-design development and usability evaluation of the system prototype was published in JMIR Rehabilitation and Assistive Technologies, together with researchers at Sahlgrenska Academy (University of Gothenburg) and the University of Borås.

Munoz-Novoa M, Guo L, Björkquist A, Kristoffersen MB, Khorramshahi P, Sandsjö L, Alt Murphy M. A Smart Textile Biofeedback Training System for Upper Limb Rehabilitation After Stroke: Co-Design Development and Evaluation Study. JMIR Rehabil Assist Technol, 2026. doi:10.2196/77999
